PROMPT:

and provide the final answer.

A bat and a ball cost $1.10 in total.

The bat costs $1 more than the ball.

How much does the ball cost?

RESPONSE:
Let's break down the problem step by step:

Step 1: Define variables
Let x be the cost of the ball.
Since the bat costs $1 more than the ball, let y = x + $1 be the cost of the bat.

Step 2: Set up the equation
The total cost of the bat and the ball is $1.10:
x (cost of the ball) + y (cost of the bat) = $1.10

Substitute y = x + $1 into the equation:
x + (x + $1) = $1.10

Step 3: Simplify the equation
Combine like terms:
2x + $1 = $1.10

Step 4: Solve for x
Subtract $1 from both sides of the equation:
2x = $0.10

Divide both sides by 2:
x = $0.05

So, the ball costs $0.05 (or 5 cents).

Therefore, the final answer is:

The ball costs $0.05
